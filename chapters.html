Teaching Machines: The Drive to Automate Education


Preface


The "Self-Driving Car" as a model for MOOCs. The drive to automate education.


Introduction: From Halcyon Skinner to Speak & Spell


The History of Public Education in the US and the History of Ed-tech. Bringing the Industrial Revolution to Schools. Content Delivery. Scale.


Chapter 1: BF Skinner and Programmed Instruction


Teaching Machines versus Testing Machines (Sidney Pressey versus BF Skinner). Behaviorism (in education and in technology).


Chapter 2: Building the Intelligent Human / Building the Intelligent Machine


Intelligence testing. Analogies and AI. The Turing Test. Intelligent Tutoring Machines (PLATO)


Chapter 3: Robots, Labor, and Learning


Rossum's Universal Robots. Taylorism. Efficiency. Baumol's Cost Disease. Automated essay graders.


Chapter 4: Personalization and the Quantified Student


Algorithms. Data. Adaptive learning. Predictive modeling. Privacy and development of the self. Freedom and agency.


Conclusion: Cyborgs and the Children's Machine


Seymour Papert. Donna Haraway. Mechanization of humans and humanization of machines.

Author

Audrey Watters is an education writer, a recovering academic, a serial dropout, a rabble-rouser, and some days, ed-tech's Kassandra.
